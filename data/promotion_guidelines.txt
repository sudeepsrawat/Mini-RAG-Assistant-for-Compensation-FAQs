POLICY FRAMEWORK AND PHILOSOPHY
Promotions within our organization are merit-based advancements that recognize exceptional performance, demonstrated capabilities at the next level, and alignment with business needs. We maintain a structured, transparent process designed to ensure fairness, consistency, and objectivity in all career progression decisions. Role changes, whether promotional or lateral, are strategic moves that support both individual career growth and organizational effectiveness.

DETAILED PROMOTION ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
1. Minimum Time-in-Role Requirements:
Standard Minimum: 12 months in current position

Accelerated Consideration: 9 months allowed with written VP approval

Exceptional Circumstances: 6 months for critical business needs with SVP+ approval

Time Credit: Prior relevant experience may count toward minimums with HR validation

2. Performance Requirements:
Consistent Performance: Minimum "Strong" rating (exceeds expectations) in last 2 performance cycles

No Active PIPs: Cannot be on a Performance Improvement Plan

Documented Achievements: Clear evidence of impact beyond current role expectations

Peer/Stakeholder Feedback: Positive 360-degree feedback on collaboration and leadership

3. Capability Demonstration:
Next-Level Competencies: Evidence of performing at the higher level for 6+ months

Skill Assessment: Demonstrated proficiency in 80%+ of next-level requirements

Leadership Readiness: For management roles, evidence of mentoring or informal leadership

Technical Mastery: For individual contributor roles, demonstrated expertise and innovation

4. Business Justification:
Strategic Need: Clear alignment with department/organizational goals

Succession Planning: Fits within established succession plans

Market Competitiveness: Retention of high-potential talent

Operational Requirements: Critical skill gaps or expansion needs

5. Budgetary Considerations:
Approved Headcount: Position must be in approved organizational chart

Salary Budget: Department has allocated funds for salary increase

Total Cost: Consideration of all compensation elements (base, bonus, equity)

ROI Analysis: Business case demonstrating value of promotion

COMPREHENSIVE PROMOTION PROCESS
Phase 1: Nomination & Initial Review (Weeks 1-2)
Nomination Windows:

Spring Cycle: March 1-15 (for April promotions)

Fall Cycle: September 1-15 (for October promotions)

Mid-Year Exception: Continuous submission with extra approvals

Nomination Methods:

Manager-Led: Formal recommendation with business case

Employee-Self Nomination: Through career development portal

Talent Review: Identification through quarterly talent calibration

Documentation Requirements:

Completed promotion nomination form

Performance documentation (last 2 reviews)

Project portfolio demonstrating next-level work

Stakeholder impact statements (minimum 3)

Phase 2: HR Validation & Calibration (Weeks 3-4)
HR Checklist:

Eligibility criteria verification

Compensation analysis (compa-ratio, market positioning)

Performance history review

Budget confirmation

Calibration Committee Composition:

HR Business Partner (chair)

Department Head

2-3 peer managers at same/higher level

Diversity & Inclusion representative

Assessment Criteria (Scored 1-5):

Performance consistency (30%)

Next-level capability demonstration (40%)

Business impact (20%)

Leadership potential/cultural alignment (10%)

Phase 3: Formal Assessment (Weeks 5-6)
Assessment Methods by Role Type:

Individual Contributor Roles:

Technical assessment or case study

Portfolio review presentation

Peer feedback collection

Management Roles:

Leadership simulation exercise

Team scenario assessment

360-degree feedback analysis

Business case development exercise

Specialist Roles:

Subject matter expert interview

Technical demonstration

Industry knowledge assessment

Phase 4: Decision & Implementation (Weeks 7-8)
Approval Authority Matrix:

CL1-CL3 promotions: Department Head + HRBP

CL4-CL5 promotions: Director + HR Director

CL6-CL7 promotions: VP + Head of HR

CL8+ promotions: SVP + Compensation Committee

Offer Components:

Salary Increase: 8-15% (based on market position and performance)

Bonus Target Adjustment: Immediate alignment with new level

Equity Consideration: For CL5+ roles, reviewed quarterly

Effective Date: 1st of month following approval

ROLE CHANGE GUIDELINES (LATERAL MOVES)
Lateral Move Framework:
Definition: Movement to a different role at same career level
Timing: Can occur any time based on business need
Approval Requirements:

Current manager approval (release)

New manager approval (acceptance)

HR validation of level alignment

Compensation Impact:

Standard: No salary change unless significant responsibility difference

Market Adjustment: Up to 5% if moving to higher-demand skill area

Geographic Change: Location differential applied if applicable

Transition Support:

30-day overlap period when possible

Formal handover documentation

Buddy system for first 90 days

Training budget allocation for new skills

MID-YEAR PROMOTION PROCESS
Special Circumstances Allowing Mid-Year Promotion:
Critical Business Need: New strategic initiative requiring immediate leadership

Succession Emergency: Unexpected departure of key personnel

Exceptional Performance: Documented extraordinary impact

Market Retention: Competing offer situation with high-value employee

Additional Requirements:
Stronger Justification: Comprehensive business case required

Higher Approvals: SVP+ level approval mandatory

Budget Verification: Mid-year budget adjustment approval

Communication Plan: Careful messaging to team/organization

COMPENSATION IMPACT DETAILS
Salary Positioning:
Minimum Increase: 8% (exceptional circumstances only)

Typical Range: 10-12% for standard promotions

Maximum: 15% (requires special approval)

Placement: Within new pay band, typically at 85-95% of midpoint

Other Compensation Elements:
Bonus Target Adjustment:

Immediate effect on next bonus cycle

Pro-rated calculation if promotion mid-year

Formal notification within bonus system

Equity Considerations:

CL5+ Roles: Eligibility for equity refresh

Grant Timing: Next quarterly equity cycle

Amount: Based on new level and impact scope

Benefits Impact:

Immediate eligibility for new level benefits

No waiting periods for upgraded benefits

Education/training budget increase

TRANSITION AND ONBOARDING SUPPORT
First 90 Days Plan:
Month 1: Foundation

Formal orientation to new responsibilities

Stakeholder introductions

Success metrics establishment

Month 2: Execution

Regular check-ins with manager

Peer support network activation

Early wins identification

Month 3: Integration

Performance against new expectations

Development plan creation

Formal transition completion

Support Resources:
Promotion Mentor: Assigned for first 6 months

Training Budget: $2,500 for skill development

Coaching: 3 sessions with executive coach (for management roles)

Peer Network: Access to new level peer group

GOVERNANCE AND COMPLIANCE
Oversight Mechanisms:
Promotion Dashboard: Quarterly review of promotion metrics

Diversity Analysis: Annual review of promotion patterns

Appeal Process: Formal 30-day appeal window

Audit Trail: Complete documentation retention

Success Metrics:
Promotion success rate (12-month retention in new role)

Time-to-productivity in new position

Employee satisfaction with promotion process

Diversity representation in promotions

Continuous Improvement:
Annual process review with employee feedback

Benchmarking against industry best practices

Manager training on promotion guidelines

Technology enhancements to support process

These comprehensive guidelines ensure our promotion and role change processes remain fair, transparent, and aligned with both individual career aspirations and organizational strategic objectives. Regular updates ensure ongoing relevance and effectiveness.
