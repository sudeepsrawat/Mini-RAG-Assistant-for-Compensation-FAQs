STRUCTURE OVERVIEW AND PHILOSOPHY
Our salary structure establishes a systematic framework for compensation administration across eight distinct career levels (CL1 through CL8), ensuring internal equity, market competitiveness, and clear career progression paths. Each pay band features defined minimum, midpoint, and maximum salary ranges that reflect market value, role complexity, and organizational impact. This structured approach enables consistent, fair compensation decisions while maintaining flexibility for individual circumstances.

DETAILED CAREER LEVEL ARCHITECTURE
Level Definitions and Scope:
Entry/Junior Contributors (CL1-CL2):

CL1 (Associate): Entry-level positions requiring 0-2 years experience, performing tasks under close supervision with limited decision-making authority. Focus on learning and skill development.

CL2 (Analyst): Junior professionals with 2-4 years experience, working independently on routine tasks while receiving guidance on complex matters. Beginning to demonstrate specialized knowledge.

Mid/Senior Individual Contributors (CL3-CL4):

CL3 (Professional): Mid-level roles requiring 4-6 years experience, handling moderate complexity independently while consulting on sophisticated problems. Regular impact on team outcomes.

CL4 (Senior Professional): Senior individual contributors with 6-8+ years experience, serving as subject matter experts who mentor others and influence team direction through technical excellence.

Lead/Principal Contributors (CL5-CL6):

CL5 (Lead/Principal): Senior technical leaders with 8-10+ years experience, driving significant initiatives and providing thought leadership across multiple teams or domains.

CL6 (Senior Principal): Experts with 10-15+ years experience, shaping technical strategy and making decisions with organization-wide impact, often representing the company externally.

Management/Director Levels (CL7-CL8):

CL7 (Manager/Director): People leaders responsible for team performance, talent development, and operational execution within defined business units or functions.

CL8 (Senior Director/VP): Strategic leaders overseeing multiple teams or large organizations, with significant P&L responsibility and enterprise-wide influence.

SALARY PLACEMENT AND ADJUSTMENT FACTORS
Primary Determinants (Weighted Scoring):
1. Experience and Qualifications (25%):

Years of relevant industry experience

Educational credentials and certifications

Specialized technical skills and proficiencies

Proven track record of success in similar roles

2. Market Data Benchmarking (30%):

Industry-specific salary survey participation (Radford, Mercer, Option Impact)

Geographic market analysis (metro vs. non-metro differentials)

Real-time compensation intelligence platforms

Peer company benchmarking (15-20 comparable organizations)

3. Internal Equity Analysis (20%):

Comparative analysis with similar roles internally

Career level alignment validation

Compensation ratio (compa-ratio) positioning

Team and department parity considerations

4. Geographic Differentials (15%):

Tier 1 (Major Metro): +25-30% (SF, NYC, London)

Tier 2 (Large Cities): +15-20% (Boston, Seattle, Toronto)

Tier 3 (Medium Cities): +5-10% (Austin, Denver, Atlanta)

Tier 4 (Standard): Base rate (most locations)

Remote Worker Policy: Tied to primary work location

5. Performance and Impact (10%):

Historical performance ratings

Documented business impact

Critical skill retention considerations

Succession planning and leadership potential

MID-YEAR ADJUSTMENT PROTOCOL
Approved Scenarios and Processes:
Promotion Compensation:

Standard increase range: 8-15% of base salary

Placement within new band: Target 85-95% of midpoint

Effective date: 1st of month following approval

Required documentation: Promotion business case

Critical Retention Situations:

Trigger: Written competing offer + high replacement cost

Maximum adjustment: 20% or to market midpoint

Required approvals: VP+ level + HR Director

Conditions: Minimum 12-month retention agreement

Significant Responsibility Expansion:

Criteria: >30% increase in scope/impact

Typical increase: 5-10%

Documentation: Updated job description + impact analysis

Review: Compensation Committee for increases >15%

Market Misalignment Correction:

Process: Quarterly market analysis batches

Threshold: >15% below market for similar roles

Typical adjustment: To 90% of market midpoint

Timing: Quarterly implementation cycles

GOVERNANCE AND COMPLIANCE
Approval Authority Matrix:
Standard Increases (<10%): Manager + HR Business Partner

Significant Increases (10-15%): Director + HR Manager

Major Increases (>15%): VP + Head of Compensation

Exception Cases: Compensation Committee review

Annual Band Adjustment Process:
Timeline:

Q3: Market data collection and analysis

October: Preliminary band recommendations

November: Leadership review and calibration

December: Final approval and communication

January 1: Effective date for new ranges

Adjustment Factors:

Market movement indices (70% weight)

Economic indicators (CPI, ECI) (20%)

Company performance metrics (10%)

Budget constraints and affordability

Compliance and Documentation:
All salary decisions documented in HRIS

Regular pay equity audits (biannual)

SOX controls for approval workflows

Complete audit trail maintained for 7 years

Annual compliance certification required

This comprehensive salary structure ensures competitive, fair, and consistent compensation practices that support our talent strategy while maintaining fiscal responsibility and regulatory compliance.
